# PRD: Transform Stræva into a High-Value Consumer App and Website

## 1. Executive Summary

Stræva today is a thoughtful local-first prototype for gentle movement, emotional check-ins, and small wins. Its core strength is not fitness tracking; it is emotional safety, low-pressure habit support, and a softer relationship with movement.

The highest-value version of Stræva is not “another fitness app.” It should become a calm behavior-change product for people who are underserved by mainstream fitness tools: people recovering from burnout, chronic stress, low energy, life transitions, low confidence, chronic illness, neurodivergence, beginners returning to movement, and anyone who wants consistency without pressure.

The product should evolve into a cross-platform wellness app and companion website focused on:
- gentle movement planning
- adaptive daily guidance based on energy and mood
- compassionate progress tracking
- support loops without social pressure
- evidence-informed routines for recovery, consistency, and wellbeing
- a trustworthy consumer brand with strong SEO and conversion paths

## 2. Current State Assessment

## What exists now

The current repository includes:
- a polished landing page
- a working local-first PWA
- energy check-ins
- movement session logging
- wins logging
- care logging
- celebration flow
- installable offline app shell
- local export/import/reset

## Strengths

- Distinct emotional positioning versus mainstream fitness apps
- Strong visual identity and editorial softness
- Low technical complexity in the prototype
- Immediate usability with no signup friction
- Clear concept coherence across app and website

## Current weaknesses

- No account system, sync, or cross-device continuity
- No personalized coaching or adaptive recommendations
- No meaningful retention loop beyond self-logging
- No outcome-driven product structure
- No monetizable premium value yet
- No health integrations or validated data signals
- No acquisition engine on the website beyond concept messaging
- Current name is likely too close to Strava and should not ship unchanged

## 3. Product Vision

Create the leading “gentle progress” platform for people who want to feel better, move more consistently, and rebuild trust with their body without pressure, shame, or performance obsession.

## Product promise

“Meet yourself where you are, and still move forward.”

## 4. Target Users

## Primary segments

### A. Burnout and overwhelm recovery users
People who want structure and momentum but cannot tolerate demanding wellness products.

### B. Low-energy movement beginners
People who want to restart movement with tiny steps and emotional safety.

### C. Chronic illness / pain / fluctuation-aware users
People whose capacity changes day to day and who need adaptive planning.

### D. Neurodivergent users
People who benefit from lower-friction routines, sensory-soft UX, and flexible definitions of success.

## Secondary segments

- postpartum recovery users
- therapy-adjacent self-care users
- older adults beginning movement again
- people using walking and low-intensity activity for mental health

## 5. Core Jobs To Be Done

Users hire Stræva to:
- help them start moving when energy is low
- make small effort feel valid and visible
- reduce guilt around inconsistency
- translate mood and energy into realistic daily action
- support long-term consistency without pressure
- provide a calmer alternative to competitive fitness apps

## 6. Strategic Positioning

## Positioning statement

For people who want wellbeing and consistency without pressure, Stræva is a compassionate movement and recovery app that adapts to your energy, validates small progress, and helps you build sustainable routines.

## Key differentiation

Unlike fitness trackers that optimize performance, Stræva optimizes self-trust, consistency, and felt wellbeing.

## 7. Product Principles

- Compassion over pressure
- Adaptation over rigid plans
- Consistency over intensity
- Reflection over raw metrics
- Support over competition
- Calm UX over dopamine-maxing patterns
- Privacy and trust by default

## 8. High-Value Product Direction

## The future product should have 4 value pillars

### Pillar 1: Adaptive daily guidance
The app should recommend a realistic next step based on:
- self-reported energy
- mood
- previous activity
- streak fragility
- user preference
- available time
- physical constraints

### Pillar 2: Meaningful progress system
Progress should shift from “distance / calories / speed” to:
- consistency score
- care score
- recovery score
- confidence score
- rhythm and momentum trends

### Pillar 3: Guided programs
Offer structured, high-value tracks such as:
- Get moving again after burnout
- 14 days of gentle momentum
- 30 days of calm walking
- Move through low-energy days
- Build a soft morning reset
- Rebuild routine after life disruption

### Pillar 4: Real support ecosystem
Build non-performative support via:
- private encouragement circles
- coach / therapist / partner check-ins
- gentle accountability
- optional weekly reflections

## 9. Product Scope

## MVP2 / Version 1.0

### Must-have app features

#### 1. Accounts and sync
- email/password or passwordless auth
- private cloud sync across devices
- onboarding with goals, constraints, and preferences

#### 2. Adaptive daily check-in
- energy
- mood
- pain / stress / capacity slider
- time available
- “what feels possible today?”
- daily recommendation engine

#### 3. Guided action system
- suggested sessions by energy band
- categories: walk, stretch, mobility, rest ritual, reset, breath, household movement
- one-tap “do this now” actions
- audio-guided or text-guided sessions

#### 4. Progress and insights
- weekly rhythm summary
- effort vs recovery balance
- best conditions for movement
- “what helps you show up” insights
- streaks redesigned as compassionate continuity, not punishment

#### 5. Small wins and care log evolution
- wins become tagged behavioral signals
- care actions map to recovery and consistency insights
- gentle journaling prompts

#### 6. Content system
- library of micro-routines (2, 5, 10, 15 minutes)
- themed programs
- recovery recommendations
- coach-written reflection copy

#### 7. Support features
- private support circle
- weekly encouragement summary
- celebration prompts
- optional “nudge me softly” reminders

#### 8. Website as growth engine
- landing pages per user problem
- interactive demo
- email capture
- waitlist / onboarding funnel
- SEO content around gentle exercise, burnout recovery, low-energy movement, walking for mental health, etc.

## Post-MVP / Growth features

- Apple Health / Google Fit integration
- wearable import for passive context only
- therapist / coach companion mode
- care-plan builder
- symptom-aware recommendation system
- AI reflection assistant with strict safety boundaries
- condition-specific programs
- employer / clinic partnerships

## 10. User Experience Requirements

## Onboarding

The onboarding should identify:
- user goal
- preferred types of movement
- current energy baseline
- what makes movement hard
- whether they want reminders
- whether they want metrics visible or hidden

The onboarding outcome should generate a personalized “gentle path.”

## Daily experience

The home screen should answer:
- How are you today?
- What is realistic today?
- What would help most right now?
- What small thing could count today?

## Tone of voice

- calm
- validating
- clear
- emotionally intelligent
- non-clinical but trustworthy
- never shaming

## 15. Risks

- Brand naming conflict with Strava-like naming
- Over-indexing on “softness” without delivering concrete value
- Building generic wellness instead of a strong category wedge
- Retention risk if recommendations are not meaningfully adaptive
- Trust risk if AI guidance becomes vague or unsafe

## 16. Recommendation on Naming

Do not launch publicly under “Stræva” or any name too close to Strava.
A naming exploration should happen before launch.
Working direction: soft movement, recovery, rhythm, calm momentum, gentle consistency.

## 18. Immediate Next Steps

1. Reposition the product from concept prototype to recovery-and-consistency app.
2. Rename before public launch.
3. Rebuild the app on a scalable authenticated stack.
4. Design adaptive check-ins and recommendation logic as the core value engine.
5. Turn the website into a conversion and SEO machine.
6. Create 2–3 premium guided programs and validate willingness to pay.

## 19. Final Recommendation

The highest-value version of this product is a calm, premium, behavior-change app for people who are alienated by mainstream fitness tools.

That is a real marketable wedge.

If executed well, the app can win not by competing on tracking depth, but by owning a category that most fitness and wellness products currently serve badly: compassionate, adaptive movement for real-life energy variability.
